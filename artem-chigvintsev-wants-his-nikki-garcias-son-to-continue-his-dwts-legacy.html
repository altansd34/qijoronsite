Artem Chigvintsev Wants His, Nikki Garcia's Son To Continue His 'DWTS' Legacy

August 2024 · 3 minute read

TALENTRECAP.COM Talent Dancing With The Stars

Photo by Cindy Ord/Getty Images for SiriusXM

Reading Time: 2 minutes

Dancing With The Stars pro, Artem Chigvintsev dreams of passing his love for dance to his 3-year-old son, Matteo. In a recent interview, he shared how unexpected but joyful fatherhood is, especially seeing Matteo comfortable on the dance floor. Chigvintsev wants this passion to continue, maybe even with all their kids joining in.

Ahead of Whitney Houston Week on DWTS, the pro dancer shared his desire to pass down his dance legacy to Matteo, whom he shares with his wife Nikki Garcia.

“First of all, I never thought I’d be a dad, then I never thought I would have a son,” Chigvintsev said. “He’s part of the crew. I feel like the dance legacy needs to continue and hopefully, it’ll better the juniors. Then, who knows, all of our children will be actually doing it right.”

Regarding expanding the family, Chigvintsev humorously dodged the question, pointing out he wouldn’t be carrying the baby for nine months. Other DWTS pros like Jenna Johnson and Val Chmerkovskiy also see potential dance future for their 10-month-old son, Rome. Similarly, Daniella Karagach and Pasha Pashkov also see potential dance future for their 6-month-old daughter, Nikita.

This week, Chigvintsev and his celebrity partner Charity Lawson were thrilled about guest judge Billy Porter bringing a “breath of fresh air” to the competition. Despite not receiving high scores from previous guest judges, they pleasantly received a remarkable score of 39 out of 40.

Artem Chigvintsev Reportedly “Would Love” to Have a Daughter

Chigvintsev recently expressed his desire for a little girl to join their family. Despite playfully remarking that he won’t be carrying the baby for nine months, Chigvintsev openly shared his wish to expand their family with his wife Garcia by welcoming a daughter.

Enthusiastically, he mentioned that having a girl would be a delightful addition to their lives, sprinkling some dreams and hopes into their future. The couple’s journey has been a whirlwind, from meeting on DWTS to becoming engaged, getting married, and joyfully welcoming their son, Matteo, into the world in July 2020.

Garcia, reflecting on motherhood, expressed immense happiness and love for their little one, capturing the early days of caring for Matteo with warmth and adoration. Sharing their life’s happenings, Garcia recalled the remarkable coincidence of both she and her twin sister, Brie Garcia, giving birth within 22 hours of each other, marveling at the similarities between their sons, Matteo and Buddy Dessert.
