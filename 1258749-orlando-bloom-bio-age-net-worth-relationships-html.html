Orlando Bloom bio: age, net worth, relationships

August 2024 · 10 minute read

Orlando Bloom is an English actor best known for his role as the arrow-wielding elf, Legolas, in The Lord of the Rings trilogy. Bloom’s success in the critically acclaimed trilogy opened doors for him in the film industry. The actor has since appeared in numerous top films, casting him further into the limelight. Besides acting, Orlando’s personal life has also been the subject of much attention.

How old is Orlando Bloom? The actor was born on 13th January 1977 in Canterbury, Kent, England. Orlando Bloom age is currently forty-two years. His step-father, Harry, was an author, lawyer and, human rights activist. Bloom’s mother, Sonia, managed a foreign languages school. Harry Bloom lived in South Africa before relocating to England. He was a fierce anti-apartheid campaigner fighting against the then prevalent racial segregation. Harry passed on in 1981 when Orlando was four years old. The actor’s biological father is Colin Stone.

Orlando Bloom young years

At the age of thirteen, Orlando learned that Harry Bloom was not his birth father. The actor’s father, Colin Stone (and Samantha’s too), was a long-time family friend to the Blooms. The actor recounts how he always thought Colin was only a family friend.

The talented actor was named after his mother’s favourite composer, Orlando Gibbons. Sonia’s love for the arts made her encourage Samantha and Orlando to attend Bible reading and drama classes. The siblings soon began reading out poetry and stories in local festivals and competitions. This early exposure to theatre helped develop Orlando’s love for acting.

Early career

Orlando joined the National Youth Theatre in London at the age of sixteen after leaving St. Edmunds. After a few seasons, the actor got a scholarship to join the British American Drama Academy. He later studied at the prestigious Guildhall School of Music and Drama for three years. The school is renowned for training various famous actors such as Ben Chaplin, Ewan McGregor, and Joseph Fiennes. In 1988, the actor was cast in a minor role in the film Wilde. The one-line part marked his first appearance in a movie.

At the age of twenty-one, Orlando fell from the third floor of a building and broke his back. However, after months of rehabilitation and having to wear a back brace, the actor recovered and returned to his regular acting life. In 1999, he received the news that he had been chosen to join the cast of the epic film trilogy, The Lord of the Rings. The films were based on novels by renowned author J.R.R. Tolkien.

Orlando Bloom films

The actor has appeared in numerous films throughout his career. Here are the most notable ones.

The Lord of the Rings trilogy (2001-2003)

After recovering from his back injury, Orlando was cast to play Legolas Greenleaf. The actor was hoping to play Faramir, a human character in the trilogy. Legolas, an elf warrior, was one of the main characters in the trilogy. The films tell the story of nine warriors (an elf, a wizard, a dwarf, two humans, and four hobbits). The nine form a fellowship to save the mythical from destruction by evil forces.

The films were filmed in New Zealand over a two-year period. While filming, the nine ‘warriors’ became very close. Orlando often spent his free time surfing, snowboarding and bungee jumping. Other cast members considered him the group’s ‘daredevil.’ The films were released over three years. The Fellowship of the Ring, The Two Towers and The Return of the King were released in 2001, 2002 and 2003 respectively.

Orlando was cast in a minor role in this Ridley Scott film. He was still working on the filming of The Fellowship of the Ring at this time. In Black Hawk Down, Orlando played an army ranger who falls from a helicopter and breaks his back. The film tells the story of the 1993 Battle of Mogadishu. The actor’s role in the film was essential and earned Orlando high praise from fans. His career was beginning to take shape. Ned Kelly (2003)

After his challenging role as Legolas, Orlando continued to appear in roles that challenged him physically. In 2003, the actor co-starred in the film, Ned Kelly, an action thriller about a disreputable outlaw from Australia. Heath Ledger played the role of Kelly, while Orlando played Joe, one of the supporting characters and a member of Kelly’s gang.

Pirates of the Caribbean (2003-2017)

In 2003, Bloom landed what is perhaps his second-biggest role to date. He was cast to play Will Turner in four of the five movies in the Disney film series, Pirates of the Caribbean. The first of the five-film series was titled Pirates of the Caribbean: The Curse of the Black Pearl. The film followed Captain Jack Sparrow and the governor’s daughter, Elizabeth, who was in possession of a valuable coin. Bloom’s character, Will, is a blacksmith-turned-pirate. Fans were treated to Orlando’s first on-screen romance (with Elizabeth) in this 2003 film.

Troy (2004)

In 2004, Orlando landed another role involving swords and daggers. He was cast to play Paris, the Prince of Troy. The actor worked alongside Brad Pitt in the film. Troy tells the story of the legendary Trojan War. Paris ignites a war between the kingdoms of Troy and Sparta when he falls in love with Helen, the queen of Sparta and takes her to Troy. Agamemnon takes that as an excuse to invade Troy and exert control of the Aegean Sea.

The Calcium Kid (2004)

In 2004, the actor felt the need for appearing in more profound roles. He took on the role of Jimmy Connelly, an amateur boxer who defeats Great Britain’s greatest boxer, Pete Wright, in a fluke. When Wright injures his arm, Connelly is picked to represent Great Britain in an upcoming championship. The Calcium Kid tells Connelly’s story from being a nervous milkman to fighting a boxing champion. Around this time, Orlando also appeared in the films Kingdom of Heaven and Haven.
